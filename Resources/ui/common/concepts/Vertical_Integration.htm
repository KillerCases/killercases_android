Vertical Integration

Definition: Vertical Integration is when one company conducts multiple functions across an industry value chain. E.g. Confectionary company farms the Coca Beans, mixes the chocolate and distributes to retailers.

Backward Vertical Integration is when a company control subsidiaries that produce some of the inputs used in production of its products.

Forward vertical integration is when a company controls distribution centers and retailers where the products are sold. In general, companies are less Vertically Integrated today than they have been in the past.
